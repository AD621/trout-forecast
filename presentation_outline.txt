SLIDE 2 — WHAT WE'RE BUILDING
-----------------
Header: What We're Building
Subhead: Combine live river sensors with weather forecasts to tell anglers
what to expect
Three rows (icon + title + description):
1. Real river data — 15-minute USGS readings: discharge, water temperature,
   dissolved oxygen, turbidity at two Chattahoochee tailwater stations.
2. Live weather — Hourly Open-Meteo data: air temperature, barometric
   pressure, precipitation, and cloud cover, both historical and forecast.
3. Daily condition forecast — A Good/Fair/Poor rating per station, refreshed
   daily by a Streamlit app driven by a scheduled GitHub Actions pipeline.

SLIDE 3 — SCOPE: CONDITIONS, NOT BITE QUALITY
-----------------
Header: Scope: River Conditions, Not Bite Quality
Subhead: An important distinction we clarified in the docs before going further
Two-column comparison:
  LEFT — "We ARE predicting"
    - Whether the water is safe and comfortable to fish and wade in
    - Whether conditions sit within trout's physiological comfort zone
      (temperature, oxygen)
    - Whether flow and clarity are reasonable for the day
  RIGHT — "We're NOT predicting"
    - Whether fish will actually bite
    - Catch rates or angler success
    - No creel-survey or catch-report data exists anywhere in this pipeline
Footer note (italic): "Good" means good conditions to go fish in — not a
guaranteed catch. This framing carries through to the Streamlit UI copy.

SLIDE 7 — FEATURE ENGINEERING
-----------------
Header: Feature Engineering
Subhead: Turning raw 15-minute readings into signals a model can learn from
Four items (numbered):
1. Discharge rate of change — 1hr/3hr CFS deltas plus a stability flag
   (<200 CFS/hr). Buford is a peaking hydro plant, so the rate of change
   often matters more than the absolute level.
2. Rolling water temperature — 3hr/12hr/24hr rolling means, computed with
   time-based windows so gaps in the sensor grid don't silently skew results.
3. Season & time-of-day — Meteorological seasons and Night/Morning/
   Afternoon/Evening buckets, converted to local Eastern time since USGS and
   Open-Meteo both report UTC.
4. Stocking week flag — generate_stocking_weeks() reproduces GA DNR's weekly
   April-July schedule, the pre-Labor Day top-offs, and a fall placeholder
   week.

SLIDE 8 — COMPOSITE SCORING RUBRIC
-----------------
Header: Composite Scoring Rubric
Subhead: Station-specific point scales -> a composite score -> Good/Fair/Poor

Table 1 — Buford Dam (max 9 pts)
  Factor                | Points | Best case
  Water temperature      | 0-3    | 10-18 C
  Discharge               | 0-3    | 600-1800 CFS
  Discharge stability      | 0-1    | <200 CFS/hr change
  Dissolved oxygen          | 0-2    | >= 8 mg/L

Table 2 — Medlock Bridge (max 7 pts)
  Factor                | Points | Best case
  Water temperature      | 0-3    | 10-18 C
  Discharge (flood flag)  | 0-2    | <= 9.5 CFS normal
  Turbidity                | 0-2    | <= 5 NTU

Table 3 — Classification thresholds
  Condition | Buford | Medlock
  Good       | 7-9    | 5-7
  Fair        | 4-6    | 3-4
  Poor         | 0-3    | 0-2

Footer note (italic): Missing readings score half credit — never zero, never
full — so an offline sensor never unfairly tanks (or inflates) a day's
rating.
